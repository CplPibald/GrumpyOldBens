"Fire in a crowded theater" 
    Oliver Wendell Holmes, Schenck vs United States, 1919
    Schenck was distributing anti-draft flyers during WWI
    "The most stringent protection of free speech would not protect a man falsely shouting fire in a theatre and causing a panic"
      Established "clear and present danger" doctrine
    Case was overturned in 1969 in Brandenburg vs Ohio
      Brandenburg created a new test: "Directed to inciting or producing imminent lawless action" and "likely to incite or produce such action"
