California Assembly Bill 5
    California changed the law on gig work
    Requires contract workers to be classified as employees under "ABC Test", so they can get benefits
    AB5 was the same law that Vox writers reported was a victory for workers, right before they were fired in March
    Kills music gig 
            Before the law passed, if he played a gig where he'd hire a drummer, bassist, and guitar player, 
            "I just cut (each) a check for $200. Now, I have to take that drummer, put him on payroll, W2 him, get 
            workers' comp insurance, unemployment insurance. I have to pay payroll taxes. I also have to now hire a 
            payroll company."  All to hire musicians for one just night. The paperwork alone might cost more than the music.
        https://reason.com/2020/03/11/the-law-that-killed-freelance-work/
        Reason contacted all 75 law professors, political scientists, sociologists who signed a letter in support of the law
            to ask what they thought now that it's killing jobs.  Zero agreed to an interview.
    California Assemblywoman Lorena Gonzales who put forth the bill when asked if she felt bad for the cut jobs, said they weren't "real jobs"
    Industries with Exceptions: 
        contractors must set their own prices, communicate directly with customers, and earn twice the minimum wage
        Doctor, dentist, psychologist, insurance agents, stockbrokers, lawyers, accountants, engineers, real estate
        Independent truck drivers protested in Sacramento.  Got another exemption.
    Uber and Lyft chose to ignore the law and go business as usual
